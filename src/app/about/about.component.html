My Coding Journey

In 5th grade, I logged into Code.org for the first time, gaining my first ever introduction to coding.

In 7th grade, I started learning the basics of web development with HTML, CSS, and Javascript through CodeAcademy.

In 9th grade, I attended my first hackathon, rapidly boosting my interest in coding. I was encouraged to learn the basics of iOS development and started to gain proficiency throughout the year.

In 10th grade, I started to extend my interest through starting to work on my own projects, mostly iOS applications. I also decided to pursue this academically, enrolling AP Computer Science and deciding to follow my school's CS pathway.

In 11th grade, I attended more hackathons and even won one early in the year. I started to experiment with more complex technologies, attempting to integrate machine learning and databases with my mobile applications.

In 12th grade, I decided to transition this interest into a career, deciding to enroll at UC Davis for Computer Science. This was when I gained my first professional experience with coding as a Web Development instructor at a local tutoring center.

In my 1st year of college, I took many introductory classes and furthered my conceptual programming knowledge. During this year, I first started applying to internships and was able to secure my first SWE internship at Chevron.

In my 2nd year of college, I continued with intermediate level computer science courses and took an introductory statistics course for fun. I enjoyed that course so much that I decided to pursue statistics as an additional major. It was this year that I started to gain a strong interest in Data Science and Machine Learning.

In my 3rd year of college, I am taking many advanced CS and Statistics courses, building on my expertise in these domains. Furthermore, I've gained a significant interest in web development and trained myself to be a full-stack developer.
